Slow is a symptom, not a cause. A calm, ordered triage finds the real bottleneck faster than reaching for a reimage.

## Ask what changed

A machine that was fine yesterday points at something recent: a new program, a pending update, or a failed process. Start there.

## Look at resources

Open Task Manager and check which resource is pinned: CPU, memory, or disk. One process at the top of the list is often the entire story. Disk at one hundred percent on an older machine is a very common culprit.

## The quick wins

- A reboot if the machine has been up for weeks
- Closing browser tabs and startup apps that eat memory
- Confirming an update or scan is not running in the background

## Escalate with evidence

If hardware is the limit, an aging drive or too little memory, document what you saw in Task Manager. A ticket that says disk pinned at one hundred percent for ten minutes gets acted on faster than one that says slow.
